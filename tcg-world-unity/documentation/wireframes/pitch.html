The Opportunity

Fact

There's a massive wave of creators building their own indie trading card games.

Fact

Players expect to be able to play and test games online—not just wait for a physical release.

The Current Solution: Tabletop Simulator

Creators use TTS because it's a sandbox with no coding required—you drag cards, build decks manually, and play by enforcing rules yourself.

TTS is popular but deeply limited:

  • No built-in deckbuilders
  • No persistent user accounts
  • Visual polish requires scripting + 3D skills
  • No way to monetize your game

The Alternative Platforms?

Some web-based platforms exist with TCG-specific tools, but:

  • They're 2D and visually flat
  • Hard to use
  • No monetization

One exception: Dulst, which does allow monetization—but:

  • It's still 2D
  • Limited to 2-player games
  • Unknown to most creators

The Problem

Developing a polished digital TCG client is simply not feasible for most indie creators.

They don't have Unity skills. They can't afford devs.

What is feasible?

  • Building a community
  • Running a Kickstarter
  • Printing cards

That's why they flock to TTS or do physical-only releases. It's the only path that doesn't require software development.

A platform where creators can build, host, and monetize their game—without writing code.
